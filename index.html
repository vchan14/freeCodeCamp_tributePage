Solin Chan

The man who educates hundred thousands of Cambodian Children

Here is a time line of Mr. Solin's career:
  • 1963 - born in Kandal province to a working-class family.
  • 1975 - went through Cambodian Genocide for 3 years and 8 months. Didn't receive any education and had to work forcefully in agriculture field with limited amount of foods.
  • 1979 - continuted his high school education for 3 years at Bak Tok High School and was one of the top graduates in his year.
  • 1990 - graduated from Royal Universiy of Phnom Penh with Bachelor Degree of Arts, major in Chemistry
  • 1990 - secretariate general at Ministry of Education. Designed and developed pre-service teacher training programme for primary and secondaryschool teacher for 10 years with supports from Save The Children Australia, USAID, and ADB.
  • 2001 - coordinated EFA planning process with United Nations agencies, NGOs Education Partnership and 16 government ministries by working closely with consultants and six-goal designated working groups in the development of EFA plan for 3 years.
  • 2001 - program director at World Education. Managed a USAID funded programme called OPTIONS-Preventing Child Labour and Trafficking, in which effective components were mainstreamed into Ministry of Education Programming. Remarkably, Led documentation of three key handbooks during my time with World Education namely "Girl's Counsellor" and "Local Life Skills Education" that contribute to Child Friendly School initiatives, and "Commune-based Livelihood for Out of School Youth" that contribute to non-formal education.
  • 2014 - present - eucation officer at UNICEF. Support the Ministry of Education to strengthen child friendly school implementation with focus on life skills that oriented students on employment skills as well as supporting quality and learning component in relation to curriculum development, teacher training and student assessment
